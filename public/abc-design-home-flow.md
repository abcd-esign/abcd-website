# ABC Design — Home Page Flow

Source: ABCD Capabilities Overview deck (2026)
Purpose: Section-by-section flow to inform the design and copy of abc.design's home page. Each section below maps to one slide in the deck.

---

## Section 1 — Hero / Cover

**Slide reference:** Cover

**Role on page:** Top of the home page. First impression. Establishes the brand and sets a confident, minimal tone.

**Content:**
- Eyebrow / wordmark: `ABC DESIGN`
- Tagline label: `Capabilities overview`
- Year mark: `2026`

**Visual direction:**
- Bold brand color background (deck uses a strong blue)
- Decorative line / wave motif sweeping across the canvas
- Pixel-style logo glyph in the corner
- Generous negative space; minimal text

**Notes for web:**
- Treat this as the hero section. Replace "Capabilities overview / 2026" with a primary value-prop headline and a single CTA (e.g., "Get in touch").
- Carry the wave motif through the page as a visual through-line.

## Section 4 — What We Offer (Process Comparison)

**Slide reference:** What We Offer

**Role on page:** Explains *how* ABC delivers faster — by visually comparing the ABC process against a traditional one.

**Content:**
- Section label: `WHAT WE OFFER`
- Headline: **A modern design process that gets ideas to launch in a fraction of the time.**

Two process diagrams shown side-by-side / stacked:

- **Our Product Process (ABC Design):**
  `Idea → ABC Design → Client Handoff → Engineering → Launch`
  (Compressed, fewer steps, minimal handoffs)

- **Traditional Process:**
  `Idea → Product → PRD → Design → Design Handoff → Engineering → Testing → Launch`
  (Many more steps, multiple handoffs)

**Visual direction:**
- Light/cream background
- Two parallel timeline diagrams; ABC's is short and clean, traditional is long and busy
- The visual contrast does the work — viewer should immediately see "fewer steps = faster"

**Notes for web:**
- This is a great candidate for an animated/interactive comparison on hover or scroll.
- Could be a single hero illustration that animates in.

## Section 6 — Work (Collapsed Summary on Homepage)

**Slide reference:** Work divider + all four case study slides (Dialogue, Robinhood, Affirm, Cash App)

**Role on page:** Proof of credibility, kept lightweight. The homepage should NOT display the full case studies — only high-level summaries that someone can drill into later.

**Homepage treatment:**
- Show the "WORK" section header (from the orange divider slide) as a chapter break.
- Below it, render a compact list or grid of case study summaries — one row/card per project — each containing only:
  - Client name / logo
  - One-line headline (the bolded outcome from each case study slide)
  - A "View case study" link or arrow that routes to the full case study page
- Do NOT show the full mockups, screenshots, or supporting subhead copy on the homepage. Those live on the detail pages.

**Headlines to use as the homepage summaries:**
- **Dialogue AI** — Fully redesigned Dialogue AI's website
- **Robinhood** — Grew Robinhood Gold from 1M to 3.2M members in one year
- **Affirm** — Designed Affirm's onboarding experience for businesses
- **Cash App** — Unified Cash App's banking features, centralizing ~$3B in monthly inflows

**Visual direction:**
- Keep the orange "WORK" header treatment from the deck as the section opener.
- Below it, a clean, minimal list — likely a vertical stack of rows or a tight 2x2 grid.
- Each row should feel like a teaser, not a full reveal — small client logo, headline, hover state that hints at more.

**Notes for web:**
- Sections 7–10 below (the full case study slides) are kept in this doc as reference for the detail pages, NOT the homepage.
- Each summary card links to its own dedicated `/work/[client]` page where the full content from Sections 7–10 lives.

## Cross-cutting design notes

- **Color system:** Blue (hero/who-we-are), Black (team), Cream/Light (process, secret sauce, case studies), Orange (work divider), Yellow (closing CTA). Each section is its own color block — the page should feel like a sequence of chapters.
- **Recurring motif:** The flowing wave/sine line appears in nearly every section. It's the brand's visual signature and should carry through scroll transitions.
- **Logo glyph:** A small pixelated/8-bit mark sits in the top-right of every slide. Treat it as a persistent corner element on the site (or as the favicon/header logo).
- **Typography:** Looks like a clean sans-serif (likely a geometric grotesk) with strong contrast between bold headlines and lighter body. Headlines do the heavy lifting; supporting copy is short.
- **Whitespace:** Every slide is generous with negative space. The site should resist density — let each section breathe.
